Dog Health Schedule

Health:

Observation:For a dog health check in one month, you should focus on routine at-home checks and consider scheduling a veterinary check-up if you notice any concerning signs or if your dog is a puppy or senior.

At-Home Checks:

Eyes: Look for discharge, cloudiness, or redness.

Ears: Ensure they are clean and free of odor, and check for signs of infection or irritation.

Teeth: Check for tartar buildup, gum inflammation, or missing teeth.

Skin/Body: Examine for any lumps, bumps, rashes, or signs of parasites.

Feet/Paws: Check for cuts, cracks, or ingrown nails.

Movement: Observe their gait and look for any signs of stiffness or pain.

Poop/Digestion: Note the consistency and color of their stool, and look for any signs of diarrhea or vomiting.

Water Consumption: Mtheir water intake and ensure they are staying hydrated.

Weight: weigh your dog to ensure they are maintaining a healthy weight.

Veterinary Check-Up (Monthly or as needed):

General Examination: A vet will assess your dog's overall health.

Vaccinations: Ensure your dog is up to date on vaccinations.

Deworming: Discuss deworming needs with your vet.

Diet Changes: Discuss any dietary concerns or changes with your vet.

Grooming: Get advice on proper grooming practices.

Health Problems: Address any specific health concerns or symptoms you've noticed.

Puppy Care:

Puppies may need more frequent vet visits for vaccinations, deworming, and health checks.

Discuss vaccination schedules and deworming protocols with your vet.
