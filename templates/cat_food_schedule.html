Cat Feeding Schedule

General Recommendations:

Kittens: Need 3-4 meals a day for rapid growth.

Adult Cats: Prefer 2 meals a day, but some like smaller, frequent meals.

Senior Cats: Maintain adult feeding routine unless advised otherwise.

Factors to Consider:

Age: Younger cats need more food per pound.

Activity Level: Active cats need more food.

Health Status: Special conditions may require dietary changes.

Reproductive Status: Spayed/neutered cats may need fewer calories.

Food Type: Wet food should be consumed quickly to prevent spoilage.

Tips for Creating a Feeding Schedule:

Establish a Routine: Helps avoid begging.

Adapt to Lifestyle: Choose a schedule that suits both you and your cat.

Monitor Food Intake: Leaving food out all day can lead to obesity.

Remember:

A balanced diet is essential for your cat's health.

Monitor weight, hydration, and adjust feeding as needed.

Consult a veterinarian if unsure about the diet.
