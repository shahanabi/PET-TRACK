Cat Health Schedule

Daily Observation:

Behavior: Watch for any changes in your cat's usual behavior, such as lethargy, reduced activity, excessive grooming, or changes in litter box habits.

Appetite and Water Intake: Note any changes in appetite or water consumption.

Vomiting or Diarrhea: Be aware of any signs of digestive upset.

Monthly Health Checks:

Eyes:Check for discharge, redness, or cloudiness.

Ears: Gently bend back the ear and look inside for any signs of infection, mites, or excessive wax.

Mouth: Lift the lips to check the teeth and gums for any signs of inflammation, tartar buildup, or sores.

Body: Feel for any lumps or bumps, and check the fur for any signs of parasites or skin conditions.

Feet: Inspect the paws for any cuts, cracks, or signs of irritation.

Bottom: Check for any signs of diarrhea, anal gland problems, or parasites.

When to Seek Veterinary Care:

Any unusual symptoms: If you notice any of the above signs, or if you have any concerns about your cat's health, consult with a veterinarian.

Routine checkups: Even if your cat appears healthy, schedule regular checkups with your veterinarian, especially for kittens, senior cats, or cats with pre-existing conditions.

Vaccination:

For a kitten, the initial vaccination series typically involves FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia) and Rabies vaccines, with boosters given every 3-4 weeks until around 16 weeks of age, followed by annual boosters.
