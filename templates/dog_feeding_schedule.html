Dog Feeding Schedule

General Guidelines:

Twice a day: Many vets recommend feeding adult dogs twice a day, with an 8-12 hour interval between meals.

Timed feeding: Allow your dog a set amount of time (e.g., 30 minutes) to eat, then remove any uneaten food.

Treats: Dog treats should make up a small portion of your dog's daily diet (5-10% or less).

Consistency: A consistent feeding routine helps regulate your dog's metabolism and digestion.

Puppy Feeding:

Age: Puppies require more frequent feedings than adult dogs.

Breed: Large breeds may need more food and potentially more frequent meals.

Activity level: Active dogs may require more food and potentially more frequent meals.

Health:

Health conditions: Certain health issues may require special feeding schedules.

Vet recommendations: Always consult with a veterinarian for personalized feeding guidelines.

Remember:

Most adult dogs thrive on two meals a day, 8-12 hours apart. Puppies often need 3-4 smaller meals per day.
